Note: These are experimental visualizations of mathematical ideas. They attempt to show relationships between different mathematical structures, but all such attempts are necessarily incomplete. The mathematics involved (topology, modal logic, differential geometry) is deep and these demos barely scratch the surface.

What This Attempts to Show

We try to visualize:

  • How mathematical structures might relate
  • Some properties of topological spaces
  • Modal logic operators (□ and ◊)
  • Basic differential geometry concepts

Whether we succeed is for you to judge.

Mathematical Background

The demos draw from:

  • Topos theory - Following Grothendieck
  • Modal logic - Following Kripke
  • Differential geometry - Following Riemann

Our understanding is limited; our implementations more so.

How to Explore

The demos respond to interaction:

  • Click to create changes
  • Type to see text analysis
  • Adjust controls carefully

What emerges may be interesting, or it may be noise.

Technical limitations: These visualizations are approximations at best. Real mathematical structures are far richer than what any computer visualization can show. We use simplified models, heuristic algorithms, and visual metaphors that may mislead as much as they illuminate.
